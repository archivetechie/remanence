# ST-HARVEST report — Linux `st` semantics against frozen TIO-5

**Status: COMPLETE — decision-oriented harvest, 2026-07-10.**  
**Target:** HPE MSL3040, half-height LTO-9, smartpqi, 1 MiB command cap, fixed 256 KiB/4 KiB records, single initiator, no partitions or drive-compression dependency.

FOLD-NOW items, ranked by rework risk:

1. **Reset/POR unit attention must invalidate the cached position and active write session.** Rem currently reserves completion-unknown treatment for transport errors and deferred sense. A current `06/29/xx` CHECK CONDITION can leave the command’s non-execution known while invalidating the drive position and mode state.
2. **Current recovered-error sense is successful completion, including recovered-error EW.** `st` deliberately treats flag-free RECOVERED ERROR as success and accepts RECOVERED ERROR + EOM as the soft-EW class. Rem currently turns both into ordinary errors.
3. **Raise synchronous data and filemark timeouts to `st`’s 900-second normal timeout.** Rem’s 60-second data and 120-second filemark windows can abort a drive while firmware is still performing recovery or buffer destage.
4. **Fixed-mode ILI must invalidate the arithmetic cursor.** TIO-5b already cancels the staged next READ and withholds the buffer, but it does not explicitly forbid reuse of the pre-ILI cached position even though the mismatched record may have advanced the tape.

**Bottom line:** the settled architecture survives intact. Deferred-error handling, one-command attribution, residual accounting, typed position proofs, EW arbitration, fencing, and tripwires are generally stronger than `st`. Fold the four semantic deltas above before dispatch; the rest belongs in executable oracles, qualification policy, A1, or the cruft register.

### Drive-quirk conclusion

`st` does **not** maintain a broad per-model quirk table. Its real-world accommodation is mostly configurable flags: omit READ BLOCK LIMITS, permit multi-record backspace, one/two filemarks, fast EOD spacing, logical/vendor addresses, immediate operations, SILI, read-ahead, buffering, partitions, and PF-bit fallback. For modern HPE/IBM LTO-9, the plausible classes are limited to residual correctness, filemark/EW reporting, timeout behavior, door-lock choreography, and mode-page verification—all already folded or retained as qualification oracles above.

## Timeout constant comparison

All `st` retry arguments are zero. Its explicit retry/poll mechanisms are the ten-UA readiness drain and up-to-120-second one-second NOT READY polling during blocking open.
